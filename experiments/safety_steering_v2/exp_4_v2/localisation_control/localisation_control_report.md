## TL;DR

_To fill after results land._ Possible takes:

## Mechanism reading

_To fill after results land. Three possible framings:_

- **Localisation supported.** Despite the token-count deficit working against it, `non_critical_only` produces only a fraction of the `critical_info_only` Δ. Evidence that the direction reads ethical content within the steered span, not just a generic "any prefill steering modulates flagging" signal.
- **No localisation.** `non_critical_only` and `critical_info_only` give comparable Δ. The v2 mechanism story collapses — the direction modulates flagging disposition broadly when applied to any matched-purpose prompt span. App. A.3 in the paper should lose the span-localisation framing.
- **Partial / confounded.** `non_critical_only` shows attenuated effect proportional to the per-scenario token-count deficit. Not a clean read either way.

## Open questions / follow-ups

- This control does not isolate token-count from content. A length-matched **`critical_truncated_only`** condition (steering only the first N tokens of `critical_span` where N matches `non_critical_span`) would fully control for steering dose. Out of scope here; flag as v3 follow-up.
- Distributed-bin scenarios are not addressed. The v2 distributed-bin signal (e.g. R4 ethical: 0.84 → 0.32 under critical_info_only) lies outside the localisation-control framework.
